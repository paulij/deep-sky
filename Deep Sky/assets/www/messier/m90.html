M90: Spiral Galaxy in Virgo

Magnitude 10.3. About 60 million ly away.

The galaxy has suffered "ram-pressure stripping". This is where the interaction between the galaxy and the intracluster medium, as the galaxy moves through that medium, removes the interstellar medium from the galaxy.

The galaxy, along with another in Coma Berenices has been classified as the prototype of an "Anemic Galaxy". An Anemic Galaxy has low contrast between its spiral arms and its disk. They have very few HII regions and hence few star forming regions.

Anemic Galaxies are thought to be on the way to becoming lenticular galaxies. [Further on that road are "Passive Spiral Galaxies" found in high red-shift galaxy clusters.]

There is still star formation going on in the center of M90.
