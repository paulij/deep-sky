M35: Open Cluster in Gemini

Magnitude 5.3 lying just off the "left foot" of Gemini. This is a really fine open cluster for a small telescope.

M35 is approximately 2,800 ly away. There are about 20 stars down to magnitude 10, 120 down to magnitude 13 and 2,700 stars down to magnitude 21 which could be part of the cluster. The cluster contains a prominant arc of stars to the north of the cluster with a bright double star at the north end of the arc.

NGC 2158 is a nearby cluster which is much more compact, older and farther away - 12,000 ly away and about 2 billion years old. It has survived for this long because it is so compact. In fact it was once thought to be a globular cluster. NGC 2158 is sen to the bottom right of M35 as a faint smudge in a small telescope but is quite prominant in the above image.

The earliest spectral type of stars in the M35 cluster are B6 and B7. This corresponds to an age for the cluster of around 100-150 million years old.
