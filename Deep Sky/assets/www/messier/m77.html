M77: Spiral Galaxy in Cetus

Magnitude 8.9. It is an active galaxy with an Active Galactic Nucleus (AGN) and is an example of a Seyfert galaxy. A Seyfort galaxy is one of a number of different AGNs including radio galaxies, quasars and BL Lacertae galaxies. All are possibly quite similar, the differences being due to their orientation to us and different rates of matter falling towards a central object.

A central object of about 100 million solar masses attracts matter and emits a large aout of radiation right across the spectrum. Gas clouds are being blown from the central regions of the galaxy at several hundred km/s.

Infrared observations show a central region about 12 ly This is surrounded by an elongated structure 100 ly across consisting of stars and interstellar matter.

Radio observations have found a central disk 5 ly across containing water molecules.

Ultra violet observations has shown intense star forming regions surrounding the central object. This are the brightest star forming regions with 100 million ly from us.

At about 60 million ly away it is a similar distance to the Virgo galaxies, but in another direction obviously.

The galaxy is very large with a diameter of 170,000 ly.

The image is a composite. X-ray data from the Chandra X-ray Observatory are shown in red, optical data from the Hubble Space Telescope in green and radio data from the Very Large Array in blue.

M77 is found just east of delta Ceti and not far from the famous variable star Mira.
