M104: Sombrero Galaxy in Virgo

Edge on spiral galaxy in Virgo, 28m ly away. Magnitude 9.0. The galaxy has a large bulge and a prominent dust lane.

The nucleus of the galaxy is designated a a LINER region (low ionization nuclear emission region). This means it contains weakly ionized gas, gas that has lost relatively few electrons. LINER regions can be powered either by star forming regions or an active galactic nucleus(AGN). In the case of ths galaxy it is most probably caused by an AGN around a known supermassive black hole. The supermassive black is one of the largest known at about a billion solar masses.

In addition the Sombrero Galaxy has a large number of Globular Clusters, between 1200 and 2000. The ratio of the number of globular clusters to size of the galaxy is high compared to the Milky Way but average for galaxies with a large bulge. The number of globular clusters around a galaxy is now thought to be related to the size of the galaxies bulge.

It is unclear whether the galaxy is part of a formal galaxy group. it has been suggested that it is one of a galaxy pair with UGCA 287.
