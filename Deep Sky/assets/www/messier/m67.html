M67: Open Cluster in Cancer

Magnitude 6.1. Over 500 stars. 3000 ly away with a diameter of 21 ly.

Between 3.5 and 5 billion years, M67 is old one of the oldest known open clusters. It is certainly much older than any other open cluster in the Messier Catalogue. It is very rare for open clusters to survive this long. One of the reasons for its survival is its distance from both the galactic center and galactic plane. This means there are less gravitational interactions which would tend to pull the cluster apart. At an age of 5 billion years, cluster stars would be roughly the same age as our sun.

M67 contains many red giants as there has been plenty of time for stars to enter their red giant phase. It also contains about 30 blue stragglers whose origins are not well understood. (Blue stars that formed when the cluster formed would long ago have gone supernova.)

The cluster is a couple of degrees west of the star alpha Cancri (Acubens).
