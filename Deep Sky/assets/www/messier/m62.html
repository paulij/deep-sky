M62: Globular Cluster in Ophiuchus

Magnitude 6.5. About 22,000 ly away and about 100 ly across.

A very irregular shaped globular cluster, probably caused by the fact that it is very close to the galactic center.

Contains a large number of variable stars, over 200. Also contains six millisecond pulsars at least three of which form binary systems with white dwarfs.

The core is very dense and has probably undergone a "core collapse" like M15 and a number of other globular clusters.

Found at the very southern tip of Ophiuchus about 4° below M19 which in turn is about 5° east of Antares.
