M22: Globular Cluster in Sagittarius

Magnitude 5.5, one of the brightest. But because of its low latitude it is less impressive in an amateur telescope than say M13.

About 10,000 ly away, one of the closest.

It and M15 are the only 2 globular glusters in the Messier catalogue which contain a planetary nebula. (4 Globular Clusters in total around the Milky Way.)

The Very Large Array radio telescope in New Mexico has discovered 2 black holes in M22, with masses between 10 and 20 solar masses.
