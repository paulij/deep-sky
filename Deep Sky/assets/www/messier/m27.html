M27: Dumbbell Nebula in Vulpecula

Magnitude 7.4. The first planetary nebula to be discovered. Also visually the best. The Helix nebula has a slightly higher magnitude (7.3) but is larger so the surface brightness is actually lower.

[A planetary nebula consists of an expanding shell of gas blown off by a star in the lateer stages of its life, the so called red giant phase. It only lasts for a relatively brief period of time, tens of thousands of years which is very brief compared to the lifetime of the star.]

Central star is magnitude 13.5 and hot with a temperature of 85,000 K.

Estimating the age of the nebula depends on an estimate of the rate of expansion of the shell. Two different estimates give ages of 2,000/3,000 and 48,000 years.

It is also difficult to estimate the distances of planetary nebula. Estimates range from about 500 to 3,500 ly. Maybe 1,200 ly is a reasonable estimate.
