M33: Triangulum Galaxy in Triangulum

Magnitude 5.7. The third largest spiral galaxy in the Local Group.

About 3 million ly away. Diameter about 50,000 ly, half that of the Milky Way. Contains about 40 billion stars (400 billion in the Milky Way and 1 trillion in Andromeda.)

An spiral galaxy, at an angle of 54 ° to us. This means it has quite a low surface brightness and given its magnitude of 5.7, can be tricky enough to spot. Often it is easier to spot in good binoculars than say a 150mm scope.
