M82: Cigar Galaxy in Ursa Major

About 12 million ly away. The prototype "Starburst Galaxy" a galaxy with exception rates of star formation. The red in the image is glowing hydrogen being blasted from the center of the galaxy.

197 young super star clusters have been found in the starburst core by Hubble. This core has 10 times the star formation rate of the entire Milky Way. Each super star cluster contains over 100,000 stars, has about a million solar masses and is 10 to 20 ly in diameter.

This behaviour is caused by the interaction with the ten times larger M81. It is believed most of the starburst behaviour was triggered by an encounter 600 million years ago. More was triggered by a recent encounter about 10 million years ago.

The image is a six image composite using filters and capturing visible and infrared light as well light from the glowing hydrogen filaments.
