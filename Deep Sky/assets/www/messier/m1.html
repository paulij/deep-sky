M1: Crab Nebula in Taurus

The Crab Nebula is a supernova remnant recorded by astronomers in 1054 AD (SN 1054). It is the only supernova remnant in the Messier Catalogue. Magnitude 8.4. Absolute magnitude about -3, corresponding to about 1000 times the brightness of the sun.

It is about 10 ly in diameter and is about 6,300 light years away. The nebula is expanding outwards at about 1,500 km/sec.

The emission from the nebula is cause by the expanding material from the original supernova colliding with the interstellar medium and forming shock waves.

The progenitor star is a 16th magnitude neutron star. It is 10 km in diameter and is a millisecond pulsar, emitting pulses every 33 ms. It originated from a core collapse supernova. Models suggest that the original star was between and 11 times the mass of the sun.

M1 is about 1 degree north of the 3rd. magnitude star zeta Tauri so not hard to find. Not particularly impressive in an amateur telescope though.
