M9: Globular Cluster in Ophiuchus

One of the nearest globular clusters to the center of the Milky Way at 5,500ly. About 29,000ly from the earth.

Magnitude 8.4. Suffers about one magnitude of interstellar absorption due to the plane of the Milky Way.

Found about 2 ° east and 3° south of the 2.4 magnitude Eta Ophiuchii (Sabik)
