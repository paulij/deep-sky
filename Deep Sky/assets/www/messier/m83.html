M83: Southern Pinwheel Galaxy in Hydra

Face on spiral galaxy about 15 million ly away. Six supernovae have been sighted in M83.

Part of the Centaurus A/M83 Group. This has two sub-groups, with Centaurus-A at the center of one and M83 at the center of the other. Both are members of the Virgo super-cluster.

The two sub-groups are close to one another and not moving relative to one another.

M83 is undergoing more rapid star formation that the Milky Way.
