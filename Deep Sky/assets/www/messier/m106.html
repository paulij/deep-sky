M106: Spiral galaxy in Canes Venatici

Magnitude 9.1. Distance 24m ly away. The galaxy has substantial star formation going on in its spiral arms.

The galaxy is classified as a Seyfert II galaxy. This means the center is a type of active galactic nucleus (AGN) seen across the spectrum from radio to X-ray. AGNs are caused by matter falling in to a supermassive black hole.

The galaxy is also classified as a Maser Galaxy. A Maser (Microwave Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation) corresponds to a Laser but in the microwave radiation band.

The image is a composite image. Optical data from the Digitized Sky Survey is shown as yellow, radio data from the Very Large Array is shown as purple, X-ray data from Chandra is shown as blue, and infrared data from the Spitzer Space Telescope is shown as red. Radio and X-ray (purple and blue) show two "arms" not visible in optical light. These extra arms are due to gas being violently heated by shock waves generated by jets coming from the AGN at the center of the galaxy.
