M103: Open Cluster in Cassiopeia

At between 8,000 and 9,200 ly away it is the one of the most distant open clusters. The difficulty in pinning down the exact distance is due to the cluster being in the band of the Milky Way and uncertainty about the amount of light obstruction.

At apparent magnitude 7.4, the cluster is visible in binoculars.
