M19: Globular Cluster in Ophiuchus

Magnitude 6.7. Distance of 45,000 ly, well beyond the galactic center. Large with mass of 1.5 million solar masses.

The second most luminous globular cluster after Omega Centauri.

About 27,000 light-years away, diameter 60 ly and contains over 100,000 stars.
