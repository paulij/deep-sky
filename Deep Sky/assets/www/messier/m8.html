M8: Lagoon Nebula in Sagittarius

Giant interstellar Cloud. Magniture 6.0. It is an Emission Nebula/ HII region.

An active star forming region. Ionization is due to a number of very luminous stars. In particular 9 Sagitarii is an O4 star with absolute magnitude -10.7, one the most powerful star of all. Recently it has been discover to be a binary. A number of other magnitude 6 and 7, really luminous O type stars also ionize the nebula.

Distance is about 4300 ly. Size 115ly x 50ly. This would dwarf the Orion nebula.

Southwest of 9 Sagitarii is the brightest part of the nebula, known itself as the Hourglass nebula. The Hourglass nebula is very young, maybe only 10,000 years old.

The nebula contains a number of Bok globules (dark, collapsing clouds of protostellar material). These contain new stars in the final stages of formation.
