M51: Whirlpool Galaxy in Canes Venatici

Magnitude 8.4. One of the show-piece galaxies for amateurs, located in Canes Venatici. In fact there are a pair of galaxies. Distance 37 million ly.

Main Galaxy (NGC 5194):

Diameter of the disk of the disk is 86,000 ly. 160 billion solar masses. So slightly smaller than the Milky Way. Lots of star forming regions in the spirals caused by interaction with the companion galaxy.

Companion Galaxy(NGC 5195):

Simulations have suggested that 5194 came through the main galaxy from behind about 500 million years ago and then passed back trough the main galaxy about 50-100 million years ago. Presently it is to the side and behind the main galaxy.

Part of the M51 group that also contains M63 and in total at least 8 galaxies, 5 of which are spirals.
