M64: Black Eye Galaxy in Coma Berenices

Magnitude 8.5. Class Sb. Named because of the dark structure on one side of the galaxy.

Recently it has been discovered that the gas and dust in the outer parts of M64 rotate in the opposite direction to both gas and dust in the inner regions. All the stars in the galaxy rotate in the same direction as the gas in the inner region. The inner region has a radius of approximately 3,000 ly while the rest extends for a further 40,000 ly (radius).

This bizarre behaviour is believed to have been caused by a collision with another galaxy that has either been absorbed by M64 or dissipated.

M64 is a degree to the north east of 35 Comae. It is about 6° south of the north galactic pole (perpendicular north of the Milky Way disk.)
