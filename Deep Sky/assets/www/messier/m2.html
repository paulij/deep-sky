M2: Globular Cluster in Aquarius

Magnitude 6.4. Type II globular cluster, i.e. very concentrated. Globular clusters are classified from class I to class XII (or 1 to 12). Class I are very concentrated while class XII are very loose with little concentration of star towards the center of the cluster.

M2 is about 40,000 ly away which is beyond the galactic center. At about 190 ly in diameter it is one of the largest known globular clusters. Contains about 1,500,000 stars and has a total mass about 900,000 times that of the sun.

M2 forms a large right-angled triange with alpha and beta Aquarii.
