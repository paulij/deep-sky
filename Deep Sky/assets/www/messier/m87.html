M87: Elliptical Galaxy in Virgo

Magnitude 9.6. About 53.5 million ly from the Milky Way. Supergiant elliptical galaxy at the center of the Virgo cluster.

Diameter of about 120,000 ly compared to 100,000 ly for the Milky Way. But given that M87 is elliptical and not jut a disk, this make the galaxy huge. Has about 12,000 globular clusters which again is a huge number compared to our 150-200.

Has an Active Galactic Nucleus containing a supermassive black hole. This is a strong source of radiation, especially radio waves.

M87 has a jet of plasma that extends for 5,000 ly, possibly more.
