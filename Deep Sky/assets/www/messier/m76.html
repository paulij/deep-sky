M76: Little Dumbbell Nebula in Perseus

Magnitude 10.1. Distance 2,500 ly (2004) but earlier estimates based on Hubble data gave a distance of 3900. Resembles the Dumbbell Nebula M27. Hence its name. The central region is a gas torus with bubbles to the north west and south east.

Expansion velocity is between 40 and 60 km/s.

The central star is magnitude 17, with a temperature of 60,000K. It has a mas of between 0.6 and 0.9 solar masses.
