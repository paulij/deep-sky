M30: Globular Cluster in Capricornus

Magnitude 7.2. About 26,000 ly away and about 90 ly across.

Has undergone a "core collapse" like M15, M70 and possibly M62. So its core is very small. It has a half-mass radius of about 8 ly. (Approx twice the distance from us to the nearest star.)

At about -23 ° , low in the sky. A couple of degrees south and just to the east of Sadr.
